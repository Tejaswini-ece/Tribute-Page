Albert Einstein

1879 - 1955

A Tribute to the Genius Who Changed the World of Physics

Albert Einstein was born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, Germany. He was a theoretical physicist who is widely regarded as one of the most brilliant and influential scientists in history. Einstein developed the theory of relativity, which, along with quantum mechanics, forms the foundation of modern physics. He became famous for his mass-energy equivalence formula, E = mc², often referred to as the most famous equation in the world. Despite facing challenges early in life and during times of great political unrest, his relentless curiosity and profound insights reshaped the way we understand space, time, and the universe.

Major Contributions

  • Theory of Special Relativity (1905)
  • Mass-Energy Equivalence: E = mc²
  • Theory of General Relativity (1915)
  • Photoelectric Effect (for which he received the Nobel Prize in Physics, 1921)
  • Pioneering work in Quantum Theory and Statistical Mechanics

Time Line

  • 1879 – Born in Ulm, Germany
  • 1905 – Published the Theory of Special Relativity
  • 1915 – Presented the General Theory of Relativity
  • 1921 – Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics
  • 1933 – Emigrated to the United States; joined Princeton University
  • 1955 – Passed away in Princeton, New Jersey

Famous Quotes

  • "Imagination is more important than knowledge. Knowledge is limited. Imagination encircles the world."
  • "Life is like riding a bicycle. To keep your balance you must keep moving."
  • "I have no special talent. I am only passionately curious."
